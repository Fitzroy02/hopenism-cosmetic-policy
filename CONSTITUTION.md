# Constitution

## Preamble

The Hopenism Cosmetic Policy Institution exists to protect consumers, empower responsible sellers, and maintain trust in a fair cosmetic marketplace. This Constitution defines the non-negotiable principles, authority model, and amendment process that govern all institutional policy.

## Article I — Mission

1. Protect consumer health, safety, and dignity.
2. Require truthfulness in listings, claims, and seller representations.
3. Build a transparent, accountable marketplace governance framework.
4. Support responsible innovation without compromising safeguards.

## Article II — Core Principles

1. **Safety Before Scale**: No growth objective overrides safety obligations.
2. **Evidence Before Claims**: Public claims must be substantiated and auditable.
3. **Fairness and Non-Discrimination**: Policies must not produce arbitrary or identity-based harms.
4. **Transparency and Traceability**: Decisions, rationale, and ownership must be documented.
5. **Corrective Responsibility**: Errors are acknowledged, remediated, and prevented from recurring.

## Article III — Institutional Scope

This Constitution applies to:

- Marketplace seller onboarding and verification.
- Cosmetic product listing and claim standards.
- Moderation, enforcement, and incident response.
- Compliance controls, audits, and governance reviews.

## Article IV — Authority and Stewardship

1. The Governance Council interprets and applies constitutional standards.
2. Policy Owners are accountable for policy quality and implementation.
3. Safeguards cannot be waived by operational urgency.
4. Any conflict between convenience and constitutional principles is resolved in favor of constitutional principles.

## Article V — Safeguard Supremacy

1. No listing, process, or policy may bypass required safeguards.
2. High-risk categories require enhanced review and explicit approvals.
3. Identified harm triggers immediate containment and documented response.

## Article VI — Documentation and Public Trust

1. Governing rules must be maintained in clear language.
2. Material policy changes require effective dates and changelog entries.
3. Consumer-facing standards should be understandable to non-specialists.

## Article VII — Amendments

A constitutional amendment is valid only if it includes:

1. Written proposal and rationale,
2. Impact assessment (safety, fairness, enforcement implications),
3. Governance Council approval,
4. Published amendment record with effective date.

## Article VIII — Ratification

This Constitution is effective upon publication in the repository root and remains in force until superseded by a duly approved amendment.
